Q1: What problem does Lockated Tenant Management solve, and why does it need to exist now?
4
Suggested Answer:India's commercial real estate sector - 120+ tech parks, 600+ Grade A offices, 250+ malls - is running on WhatsApp groups and spreadsheets for tenant communication, service request management, and visitor tracking. A tech park with 50 tenants may have no SLA on maintenance, no audit trail for visitor access, and no unified communication channel. At the same time, the Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 has made it illegal to store tenant employee data on foreign cloud servers without explicit compliance architecture. Every major tenant experience platform (HqO, Equiem, Yardi CommercialCafe) stores data on US or EU servers, creating a structural compliance gap for any Indian landlord with BFSI, pharma, or defense tenants. Lockated exists because no other platform solves both the operational chaos and the data sovereignty challenge simultaneously.

Q2: Who exactly is the customer, and why would they pay for this?
9

Q4: What makes Lockated defensible against competition?
19
Suggested Answer:Three layers of defensibility. Layer 1 - Structural: Data sovereignty via on-premise deployment is architecturally impossible for any cloud-native competitor to replicate without rebuilding their entire infrastructure. HqO, Equiem, and Yardi have USD 100M+ invested in cloud architecture that cannot be dismantled. Layer 2 - Product breadth: Lockated is the only platform in India combining OSR ticketing, digital wallet, loyalty engine, community features, visitor management, and amenity booking in a single product built for commercial CRE. Competitors either serve residential (Mygate, NoBrokerHood) or are pure-facility-ops tools (Facilio, Building Engines). Layer 3 - Switching cost: Once a landlord deploys Lockated on their infrastructure, their operational data is on their own servers. Migrating to another vendor requires re-deploying infrastructure, re-onboarding 1,000+ users, and rebuilding historical data. The switching cost is structurally high after 12 months of deployment.

Q8: What are the biggest risks and how are they mitigated?
39
Suggested Answer:Risk 1 - Implementation complexity for on-premise deployments: Mitigated by self-service onboarding portal (Phase 1 roadmap), pre-built deployment packages for common server configurations, and a dedicated implementation team with a 4-week go-live guarantee. Risk 2 - Competition from large global players entering India: HqO has no India sales team and is cloud-only. Facilio is operations-focused and cannot pivot to tenant experience without 12+ months of product work. The DPDP compliance moat makes cloud-only entries structurally disadvantaged. Risk 3 - Customer concentration if top 3 clients represent 60%+ of ARR: Mitigated by the FM channel partner strategy which distributes deployment across 50+ properties through a single partnership relationship. Risk 4 - Regulatory change diluting data sovereignty advantage: Unlikely in short term - DPDP Act 2023 enforcement is intensifying, not weakening. Enterprise tenants (BFSI, pharma) have independent mandates beyond DPDP.
