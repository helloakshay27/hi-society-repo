Q1: What problem does FM Matrix PTW solve and how large is the market?
4
Suggested Answer:
5
FM Matrix PTW solves the failure of paper-based, email-driven, and fragmented digital tools to govern high-risk contractor work in complex organizations. Today, permit requestors use WhatsApp for approvals, safety officers rely on paper checklists with no GPS or time-stamp evidence, and vendor accountability is captured in email threads that disappear. When a contractor injury or regulatory audit happens, there is no traceable permit record. FM Matrix PTW replaces this with a configurable, mobile-ready, QR-field-executed digital permit system. The global Permit Management Software market is valued at USD 1.2 billion in 2024 and forecast to reach USD 2.5 billion by 2033 at 9.1% CAGR (Verified Market Reports 2026). India's OSH Code 2020 enforcement, Factory Act compliance obligations, and rising contractor workforces in manufacturing, logistics, and real estate create a rapidly expanding India-specific TAM.

Q2: Who is the ideal customer and what triggers them to buy?
10
Suggested Answer:
11
The ideal FM Matrix PTW customer is a mid-to-large Indian organization with 10 or more sites, a contractor workforce of 50 or more individuals, and a safety compliance obligation under the Factory Act, OSH Code 2020, or a client EHS audit requirement. Primary buyer roles are Head EHS, Plant Safety Manager, and Facility Manager. Secondary buyer triggers include the departure of a key safety officer whose paper-based process knowledge leaves with them, or a new CFO or CEO directive to reduce liability exposure.

Q3: What is the core product and what makes it defensible?
16
Suggested Answer:
17
FM Matrix PTW is a digital control-of-work platform covering the complete permit lifecycle: configurable permit taxonomy and JSA templates by permit type; vendor inbox with structured form completion and document placeholders; multi-level digital signature approval routing; QR-based geo-fenced field activation via FM Matrix mobile app; real-time safety checklists with auto-hold on negative response; permit monitoring with multi-status visibility; hold/resume with undertaking; extension management; and closure with mandatory evidence. The platform's defensibility comes from five sources: (1) QR geo-fenced field execution is absent in all India PTW competitors including Safetymint and SafetyCulture. (2) Vendor inbox with document placeholders creates a structured contractor accountability record no competitor replicates. (3) JSA templates mapped per permit type with version control is not available as standard in any India-market platform. (4) Auto-calculated validity from final approval timestamp removes the manual error found in all paper and most software alternatives. (5) The combination of mobile-field execution, configurable permit taxonomy, and India-market pricing creates a switching cost moat once customers configure their permit types and templates.

Q6: What is the competitive landscape and where does FM Matrix PTW win?
34
Suggested Answer:
35
The PTW software market in India has two competitive sets. India-built EHS platforms (Safetymint, Effivity) are affordable but shallow in PTW workflow depth - no QR field execution, no vendor inbox, no JSA-to-permit-type mapping. Global enterprise EHS suites (VelocityEHS, Intelex, Cority) have deep PTW features but cost 5-10x the price of FM Matrix PTW and require 6-18 month implementations with dedicated IT resources - pricing out India mid-market entirely. FM Matrix PTW wins in the gap: organizations that have outgrown paper permits and affordable-but-shallow India tools, but cannot justify the cost and complexity of global enterprise suites. The 15-feature comparison in Tab 4 shows FM Matrix PTW AHEAD on 7 of 15 features vs the full competitive set - including the most operationally critical: QR geo-fenced field activation, auto-hold on negative response, and vendor inbox workflow.
